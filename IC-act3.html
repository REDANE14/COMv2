1.3 PC Dissasembly

Instruction

Tips: When disassembling a PC, it's important to remember a few key tips to ensure you don't damage any components or harm yourself in the process

  1. Turn off the computer and disconnect all cables from the back, including the power cord.
  2. Open the computer case by removing the screws or clips holding the side panel in place. This will give you access to the inside of the computer.
  3. Remove any expansion cards, such as graphics or sound cards, by unscrewing the bracket holding them in place and gently pulling them out of the slot.
  4. Disconnect any power cables or data cables from the motherboard, taking care not to damage the connectors.
  5. Remove any drives, such as hard drives or optical drives, by unscrewing them from the case and disconnecting the cables.
  6. Remove any cooling components, such as a CPU cooler or case fans, by unscrewing them from the case and disconnecting any power cables.
  7. Remove the power supply by unscrewing it from the case and disconnecting any power cables.
